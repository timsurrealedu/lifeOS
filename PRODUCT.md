# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Tim, the sole user — a student/builder who wants to capture fleeting stuff (typed notes, dictated
voice, photos, handwriting) into a mobile inbox with near-zero friction, then batch-process it later
via a Claude run that files everything into an Obsidian vault (notes, Google Calendar events, TODOs,
wikilinks). Used across both phone (mostly capture, on the go) and desktop (mostly Browse/reading,
often in the evening).

## Product Purpose

lifeOS exists to separate capture from organization: dump anything into the inbox instantly, no
filing decisions in the moment, then let an AI agent do the sorting work in batch. Success = the
user never hesitates before capturing something because filing it is someone else's problem later,
and the resulting vault stays genuinely well-organized (linked, tagged, graphed) without manual upkeep.

## Brand Personality

Warm, calm, personal — "evening desk lamp," not "corporate dashboard." Terracotta-on-warm-dark by
default, editorial serif headings, quiet confidence. This is one person's private notebook, not a
product being sold to a team.

## Anti-references

Generic SaaS dashboard: card grids, blue accents, hero-metric tiles, sidebar-nav-plus-topbar
enterprise chrome. Also avoid the inverse failure — twee/pastel journaling-app cuteness. The vault is
serious personal infrastructure dressed casually, not a toy.

## Design Principles

- **Capture must stay frictionless.** Nothing in the Inbox tab should ask the user to think about
  organization — that's the whole point of deferring it to Process Inbox.
- **The AI's scratch work stays backstage.** Captures/Drafts/inbox-archive are processing plumbing,
  not content — the user-facing surfaces (Browse, Graph, Plan) should read as "my notes," not "the
  AI's workspace." (Already reflected in hiding staging folders from Browse.)
- **Warmth over sterility.** The lamplight theme is deliberate — resist pulling toward flat/cold
  "productivity tool" visual defaults.
- **Low ceremony, single tenant.** No roles, permissions, multi-user chrome, or enterprise patterns —
  every screen is a personal notebook for one person, not software being sold.
- **Legible over decorative.** It's used in the evening under a warm dark theme — contrast and
  readability win over cleverness every time.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No formal WCAG target (personal, single-user app) — but contrast and readability still matter given
regular evening/low-light use under the dark theme. No specific vision/motor accommodations needed
today.
